I am a PhD student at UC Berkeley and a member of Berkeley AI Research advised by Jitendra Malik and funded by the NSF GRFP. Currently I am doing an internship at Meta/Facebook AI Research. Previously, I was part of the inaugural group of AI Residents at Google Brain.

I did my undergraduate at the University of Pittsburgh where I double-majored in neuroscience and computer science, and minored in chemistry. During my time there I had the pleasure of working with David Koes on computational drug discovery.

Research

My current research interests include computer vision and human perception. I'm interested in how we can teach computers to see and perceive the world as a collection of objects in 3D without requiring hundreds of thousands of annotations. Plus, how can computational tools be used in novel ways to help us better understand and uncover insights about human perception?
